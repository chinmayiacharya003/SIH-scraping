<p>Hyderabad (Telangana) [India], November 5 (ANI): Amid the controversy over the alleged lapse in the <a href="/topic/kaleshwaram-lift-irrigation-project">Kaleshwaram lift irrigation project</a> on the Godavari river in poll-bound Telangana, Union Minister and Bharatiya Janata Party leader <a href="/topic/anurag-thakur">Anurag Thakur</a> on Saturday said, "Kaleshwaram is the biggest engineering blunder." <br/>The Kaleshwaram Lift Irrigation Project (KLIP) is a multi-purpose irrigation project on the Godavari River in Telangana's Kaleshwaram.<br/>Thakur said this while addressing the media at Hotel Katriya Towers, Hyderabad.<br/>The Kaleshwaram project suffered heavy damage due to massive floods last year. According to reports, one of the barrages within the Kaleshwaram barrage at Meddigadda was damaged.<br/>Leaders from both the state and central Congress have consistently alleged that KCR and his family utilised the Kaleshwaram project as an 'ATM' to syphon off Rs 1 lakh crore rupees.<br/>Meanwhile, Thakur targeted Chhattisgarh Chief Minister Bhupesh Baghel, saying, "In Chattisgarh, the CM is involved in a Rs 508 crore scam through a betting app."<br/></p><p>He further alleged, "The Chattisgarh government has become an ATM for Congress. From top to bottom, Congress is steeped in corruption. Foreign money is coming through the Mahadev app." <br/>Chhattisgarh is scheduled to hold polls in two phases on November 7 and 17. The counting of votes will take place on December 3 in all five poll-bound states.<br/>The Model Code of Conduct has come into force with the announcement of poll dates.<br/>Earlier in the 2018 assembly polls, the Congress witnessed a landslide victory, winning 68 seats out of 90 of those against the then-ruling BJP, which acquired 15 seats.<br/>The Congress and Bharatiya Janata Party are the key players in Chhattisgarh, Madhya Pradesh, and Rajasthan.<br/>Meanwhile, Telangana is going to the polls on November 30. The counting of votes will take place on December 3. The state is set to witness a triangular contest between the ruling BRS, Congress, and BJP.<br/>In the previous Assembly elections in 2018, the BRS won 88 of the 119 seats, hogging 47.4 per cent of the total vote share. The Congress finished a distant second with 19 seats and a vote share of 28.7 per cent. (ANI)<br/></p>
